Questions

FAQ

Is the camp really free?

Yes — 100% free for all students. ProtoSpark is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit and our mission is to remove every barrier to CS education.

What if my child doesn't speak English?

We are actively working to translate all curriculum materials into multiple languages. Chapter leaders in each country provide instruction in the local language. When registering, indicate your preferred language and we'll connect you with the right chapter.

What timezone are the sessions in?

Live sessions are scheduled to accommodate the largest number of participants globally. All sessions are recorded and available for asynchronous viewing. Chapter leaders also run local timezone-friendly sessions.

What do students need to participate?

A device (computer, tablet, or Chromebook) with internet access and a free Scratch account at scratch.mit.edu. No downloads or paid software required.

Can my teen volunteer even if they're not a coding expert?

Absolutely. Chapter leaders need to be willing to learn Scratch, not already know it. We provide training and all curriculum materials before camp starts.

Is ProtoSpark a real nonprofit?

Yes! ProtoSpark is in the process of finalizing its 501(c)(3) registration with the IRS. All organizational records and leadership structure are in place.

What Students Learn

From Zero to Project

Our curriculum is built around Scratch — MIT's free visual coding platform — designed so any kid can start building on Day 1.

🖼️

Backdrops

Create and customize scene backgrounds. Learn how backdrops trigger code and tell stories.

🐱

Sprites & Costumes

Add characters, paint your own, upload images, and animate with costume switching.

💨

Motion & Animation

Move, turn, glide, and bounce sprites around the stage with motion blocks.

🎵

Sounds

Add audio clips, control volume and pitch, layer sounds with code for rich experiences.

⚡

Events

Use "when clicked" and "when key pressed" to make projects interactive and responsive.

🤔

Sensing & Logic

Detect collisions, ask questions, use conditionals — the building blocks of real programming.

🏆

Capstone Project

Students combine everything they've learned to build a unique interactive project — a game, story, or animation. They present it to the whole camp on the final day.

Global Organization

One Chapter. Every Country.

ProtoSpark scales internationally through a chapter system — one chapter per country, each led by a local Chapter Leader who bridges language, time zone, and cultural gaps.

// How Chapters Work

01
Local Chapter Leader

A volunteer in each country runs their chapter, recruits local students, and coordinates with the global team on scheduling.

02
Language Bridging

Chapter leaders translate instructions and check-ins into their local language. Core curriculum materials are translated before camp begins.

03
Time Zone Management

Sessions are recorded and posted for asynchronous viewing. Chapter leaders run supplemental local sessions where needed.

04
Centralized Coordination

All chapters coordinate via shared tools. Standups twice a week keep the global team aligned.

Leader Requirements

Leadership roles are open to teens and young adults worldwide.

  • ✓ Recruit at least 3 students from your country
  • ✓ Bilingual: English + your local language
  • ✓ Basic knowledge of Scratch (or willing to learn — we provide training!)
  • ✓ Attend bi-weekly standups (Tue & Thu, 9 PM ET)
  • ✓ Passionate about making CS education accessible
